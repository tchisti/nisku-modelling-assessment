Quick reference — defaults & assumptions

Where defaults come from

  • r_max = 2.5 km → π·r² = 19.6 km² footprint (your stated bound)
  • h_net P10/P50/P90 = 39/42/46 m → Winterburn Nisku 60–70 m gross × NTG 0.65 (15-32 analog); Pine/Oak/Cedar zone 1010–1090 m
  • φ P50 = 0.078 → 15-32 Highvale FD core, 45 samples (same formation analog)
  • S_wi P50 = 0.25 → Bennion-Bachu 2008 carbonate analog (no Pine/Oak/Cedar SCAL yet — gap)
  • ρ_CO₂ P10/P50/P90 = 600/670/720 kg/m³ → PR-EOS at P_init 11 MPa / T_res 36.5°C; range reflects EOS uncertainty near critical (T_r=1.02)
  • E P10/P50/P90 = 1.5%/3.5%/6% → USDOE Atlas + WASP saline-aquifer triangle
  • BHP headroom 4.5 MPa → BHP_max (0.9 × 17.3 MPa) − P_init (11.0 MPa) at 1050 m; FG = 16.5 kPa/m
  • k = 215 mD → 15-32 Kmax geomean (45 samples, same Winterburn Nisku formation)
  • r_e implicit in Mathias = r_max from above

Sanity benchmarks

  • Per-well injectivity for 250 kt/yr/well → ΔP near-well typically 0.5–1.5 MPa at k=200 mD
  • Plume radius at 25 yr ≈ √(V/(π·h·φ·(1−Swi))) · √M, M≈8
  • WASP P50 at Nisku regional scale: ≈10 Mt/(20 km radius) at E=2.8%
  • EPA Class VI default ΔP_crit (AOR) = 1.5 MPa at base of seal

How to read the output

  • Capacity is static / volumetric — does not check whether you can inject this fast
  • Annual rate ÷ per-well rate = wells needed (Phase-1 sizing)
  • If P50 utilization > 80%, push E up via pressure management or expand footprint
