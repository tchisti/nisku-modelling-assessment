The bottom line, stated directly

✓ What works

  • Injectivity is not the problem. Real core data (15-32 Kmax geomean 215 mD × 37 m net pay) gives ~60 MPa·d/kt injectivity index — BHP stays 7+ MPa below frac pressure at 250 kt/yr/well.
  • Plume stays contained within lease. End-of-injection radius ≈ 6.2 km; post-injection drift stabilizes by 75 yr at < 7 km. Well within a 500 km² lease (radius 12.6 km).
  • MICP seal capacity 3× design column. Published Nisku caprock P_entry 6.5 MPa (brine-CO₂) supports ~190 m CO₂ column; actual column ≈ 60 m.

⚠ What is marginal or unknown

  • Capacity is marginal at default E=3%. P50 lease capacity 24 Mt vs 25 Mt commit → utilization 104%. Headroom only emerges at E ≥ 3.5% (requires pressure management / brine extraction).
  • USDW-lift AOR is binding and exceeds the lease. At k=215 mD (real 15-32 geomean), the pressure-front reaches ΔP_crit=1.0 MPa (USDW-lift) at ≈18 km — outside the 12.6 km lease radius. EPA dual (1.5 MPa) criterion gives a much tighter 5.4 km. 7 legacy wells fall inside all three envelopes and need integrity verification.
  • No 15-32 DFIT, no caprock MICP on Calmar, no 15-32 SCAL. These block FID-grade submission. See Gap Register.

Methodology

This is a Phase-1 feasibility-grade characterization — not a history-matched full-field simulation. The goal is to size capacity, injectivity, plume, and AOR to within ±30% using defensible physics and real measured inputs, and to identify exactly which data gaps block FID-grade modelling.

Workflow

  1. Anchor on measured data. 15-32 (45 Nisku samples, full-diameter + routine k-φ) provides the primary reservoir description. 8-17 (20 samples, SLBCARB XRD + TRAdata) calibrates mineralogy and provides a secondary permeability realization.
  2. Fluid properties from correlations. Span & Wagner CO₂ EOS at T_res 62 °C, P_init 19.8 MPa → ρ_CO₂ 708 kg/m³, μ_CO₂ 0.058 cP. Brine density 1062 kg/m³ (75,000 ppm TDS), μ_brine 0.45 cP.
  3. Capacity by Bachu 2008 volumetric. M = A·h_net·φ·(1−S_wi)·ρ_CO₂·E with Monte Carlo over all six inputs (10,000 realizations). Storage efficiency E sampled from USDOE Atlas triangular distribution (1%, 3%, 6%).
  4. Injectivity by Mathias 2009 radial. ΔP = (Q·μ_CO₂)/(2π·k·k_rg·h_net)·[ln(r_e/r_w) + s]. Peaceman correction for well-block geometry at FV scale.
  5. Plume by Nordbotten-Celia 2006 similarity. r(t) ≈ √[Q·t/(π·h_net·φ·(1−S_wi))] · √M for viscosity ratio M ≈ 8. Extended post-injection stabilization via gravity-dominated tongue model.
  6. Pressure front by Theis 1935. ΔP(r,t) = (Q·μ_b)/(4π·k·h) · W(u), u = r²·φ·μ_b·c_t/(4·k·t). Total compressibility c_t = 7×10⁻¹⁰ Pa⁻¹ (rock + brine).
  7. Three AOR criteria, compared. (a) USDW-lift: ΔP at seal base sufficient to push reservoir fluid past base-of-groundwater-protection; (b) Dual: max(plume, ΔP=1.5 MPa front) — EPA Class VI default; (c) Plume-only: S_CO₂ > 0.02 contour at end of stabilization.
  8. Seal capacity by MICP. H_max = 2·(P_ct − P_cr)·cos(θ)·σ_ct·σ_wc / [(Δρ)·g·σ_mct·σ_mcr]. Published Nisku caprock (Calmar / Ireton) P_entry values.
  9. Gap register + monitoring plan. Every assumed parameter ties to a specific data-collection recommendation with cost/timeline.

What is analytical vs numerical

Analytical (closed-form)

  • Capacity (volumetric, Monte Carlo via Box-Muller + inverse-CDF triangular)
  • Plume radius (Nordbotten-Celia)
  • Pressure front (Theis well function W(u), numerically summed)
  • MICP column height, Brooks-Corey relative perm

Numerical (iterative in JS)

  • Three-AOR bisection root-finders for r at ΔP_crit per criterion
  • Layer-cake Pareto disaggregation of 15-32 k-φ distribution (5-layer upscaling)
  • Fractional flow integration for front-end saturation (Buckley-Leverett)
  • USDW-lift critical ΔP back-calculation through hydrostatic column

Storage Capacity Estimation

MCO₂ = A · hnet · φ · (1 − Swi) · ρCO₂ · E    [kg]

Volumetric (static) capacity — gross pore volume available minus irreducible water, scaled by the fraction of pore volume actually sweepable by the injected plume (the storage efficiency E, typically 1–6% for open saline aquifers). This is the industry-standard "CSA Z741 / Bachu" formulation and is the first-pass capacity metric required for AER D065 and EPA Class VI submissions.

Plume Migration Analysis

r(t) ≈ √[ Vinjected / (π · hnet · φ · (1 − Swi)) ] · √M
Vinjected(t) = Q · t / ρCO₂    ;    M = (λCO₂/λbrine) = (krg/μg) · (μw/krw) ≈ 8

Nordbotten-Celia 2006 similarity solution for gravity-buoyancy-dominated radial flow. Valid for vertically integrated flow in a confined aquifer where the plume shape is approximately a Ghyben-Herzberg tongue. The viscosity ratio M>1 means CO₂ is more mobile than brine, so the plume is larger than a simple volumetric piston displacement by factor √M.

AOR Delineation — Three-Criterion Comparison

Area of Review is not a physics answer — it's a regulatory choice about which criterion defines "where does this project need to verify containment?". AER Directive 065 and EPA Class VI both allow multiple approaches; the defensible filing strategy is to compute all three and pick the binding one.

Criterion definitions

1 — Plume-only

AOR = maximum CO₂ plume extent at end of stabilization period (75 yr). Simplest to compute. Defensible only when caprock is proven intact and no legacy penetrations exist.

rAOR = rplume(t=tstab) · (1 + margin)

Weakness: ignores brine displacement pressure front, which travels faster than the plume itself.

2 — Dual (EPA default)

AOR = max(plume extent, radius where ΔP at seal base ≥ 1.5 MPa). EPA Class VI 40 CFR 146.84 default when no site-specific lift calculation.

rAOR = max[ rplume, r(ΔP=1.5 MPa) ]

Strength: captures both physics + precautionary margin.

3 — USDW-lift (site-specific)

AOR = radius where reservoir pressure is sufficient to lift fluid column from reservoir to base of lowermost USDW via a hypothetical open legacy well.

ΔPcrit = (ρbrine − ρdisp) · g · Δz
≈ (1062 − 1000) · 9.81 · 1775 / 10⁶ ≈ 1.08 MPa

Strength: physically rigorous, site-specific. Requires depth to base of groundwater protection.

MICP Data Integration — Seal Capacity

GAP No direct MICP on the Calmar / Ireton caprock above 15-32. Seal capacity is estimated from published Nisku-region caprock MICP (Bachu 2008, Kaldi & Bachu 2009) and must be verified by acquiring fresh caprock plugs on the first injector drill-through.

MICP → column height conversion

Hmax = ( Pentry,CO₂-brine − Pentry,reservoir ) / ( Δρ · g ) · (σCO₂-brine / σHg-air) · (cos θCO₂-brine / cos θHg-air)

Laboratory MICP uses mercury, which wets differently than a CO₂-brine system. Converting Hg-air threshold pressure to equivalent CO₂-brine entry pressure requires the IFT ratio (σ_CO₂-brine/σ_Hg-air ≈ 0.07) and wettability correction (cos θ ratio ≈ 0.70 for typical carbonate caprock).

Heterogeneity & Vertical Layering

The 45-sample 15-32 k-φ distribution is wildly skewed — geomean Kmax is 215 mD but the maximum is 10,240 mD, and just three samples (k>1000 mD) provide ~40% of total flow capacity (kh). This is typical Nisku reef/ramp architecture (Stoakes 2010). Ignoring it gives wrong injectivity by 2-3×.

Implications for development

Injectivity (k·h weighted)

Good — the high-perm Q4 layers provide the injectivity headroom. Single-layer completion in Q4 facies could achieve > 350 kt/yr.

Plume shape (vertical)

Challenging — CO₂ will channel into Q4 thief zones, bypassing lower-perm intervals. Pore-volume access is reduced; effective E drops to 2–2.5% from 3.5% nominal.

Monitoring design

Critical — monitoring wells must sample multiple layers; InSAR surface deformation must be sensitive to the high-perm zones specifically.

Recommendations — What to do Monday morning

  1. Commit to DFIT on the first injector. This is the single highest-ROI data-acquisition item. $180-260k. Closes σ_hmin, P_frac, permeability, pore pressure simultaneously. Any model built without DFIT carries ±15% BHP uncertainty — unacceptable for a facility that will inject at 80–90% of frac pressure.
  2. Cut caprock plugs on the first injector drill-through. Calmar + Ireton, 3-5 plugs each. Lab program (Hg-MICP + CO₂-brine direct threshold + XRD) is $80-120k. Closes containment demonstration — this is the single document that regulators will scrutinize hardest.
  3. Pull individual well records for all 7 legacy wells in AOR. Start with 100/14-22-050-03W5 (abandoned pre-1985, highest risk). Records search via AER One-Stop is free + staff time. Targeted MIT / cement-bond logs on the 2-3 highest-risk wells is $150-400k.
  4. Run CO₂-brine SCAL on 15-32 preserved core (if still in storage). 5 plugs covering 4 facies. Centrifuge + steady-state Pc + rel-perm. $200-320k. Eliminates the single largest parameter uncertainty in plume shape (S_gr).
  5. Write the pressure-management strategy. If E stays at 3% the 25 Mt commit is at 100% utilization. Either commit to pressure management (brine extraction wells, 2-3 in deeper Cambrian or Cooking Lake) or de-risk by moving to 3.5-4% E. Pressure management adds $8-15M capex but buys unambiguous AOR / capacity headroom.
  6. Start 3D seismic re-processing. The existing 3D cube on the Highvale lease was acquired for oil-and-gas exploration. Re-process for CCS — AVO, prestack, shallow-to-Nisku frequency spectrum — $400-800k. Maps faults, structural integrity, and gives a baseline for 4D monitoring.
  7. Submit a Phase-1 FEED-grade AER scheme amendment. Even though current work is pre-FID, early regulatory engagement lets AER push back on methodology now (cheap) rather than later (expensive). Budget 3-4 months for pre-consultation + draft submission.

The 80% solution vs the 100% solution

The 80% solution, deployable today: this dashboard + the 18-sheet xlsx workbook + the reference guide, used for internal stakeholder alignment, FEED scoping, and budget commitments. Sufficient for a "go to Phase-1 drilling" decision.

The 100% solution, deployable in 12 months: close the 4 Tier-1 gaps ($1.1M), run a proper CMG-GEM or TOUGH2 coupled-process simulation on the calibrated data, file FID-grade AER D065 amendment. Required for final investment, not required to start.

Do not wait for the 100% solution to commit to Phase-1 drilling. The DFIT, MICP, and SCAL programs are only possible with the first injector. Chicken-and-egg. Commit to the drilling budget, lock in the data-acquisition program within the drilling AFE, and iterate the model as data returns.
